What is the standard definition for "root cause" in flight punctuality reports?
09:15 · Ana Costa
HK
Based on Hi Fly's validated data governance documentation:

Root Cause is defined as "the primary, single-most contributing factor that directly initiated a delay or disruption event, traceable to a specific operational domain — Technical, Ground Handling, ATC, Weather, or Commercial."

Key rules to remember:
  · Only one root cause is assigned per event — the most proximate cause.
  · Contributing factors are logged separately in the secondary causes field.
  · IATA delay codes 00–99 map to root cause categories in Annex B of the Ops Manual.

📄 Source: Hi Fly Ops Data Dictionary v2.4 — validated by DataWings CoP, March 2026.
⚠ This definition is reviewed quarterly. If you notice a discrepancy, flag it in the Knowledge Curation panel or contact your DataWings representative.

Has anyone dealt with a passenger refusing to deplane during an AOG situation on a charter before?
09:17 · Ana Costa
HK
I found 2 relevant War Stories documented by experienced Hi Fly crew:

📖 WS-041 · Charter AOG, Faro (2023)
Senior Purser Rui Baptista documented a case involving 14 passengers refusing to exit a grounded A330 during a 6-hour maintenance delay. Resolution involved immediate Passenger Welfare protocol, refreshments, hotel vouchers, and a calm private conversation with the group leader. Full de-escalation took ~40 minutes.

📖 WS-019 · Charter diversion, Málaga (2022)
Crew Chief Marta Lopes resolved a similar incident by having the Station Manager board the aircraft and provide a written commitment on the new departure time.

💡 Common theme across both accounts: transparent communication with a clear timeline is the single most effective de-escalation tool.
